## What ownership means

Every strip has controller state that every client renders:

- `owner`: who currently controls the strip.
- `next_controllers`: controllers who are expected to take over next (handoff order).
- `previous_controllers`: controllers who have already held the strip.

In addition to those controller lists, the UI also renders pending handoffs from the backend coordination state:

- Normal transfers and tag requests appear as pending coordination until the server broadcasts completion (assume) or removal (reject/free).
- The frontend tracks this pending coordination in `stripTransfers` and clears it when it receives the relevant websocket broadcasts.

## How ownership is shown in the UI

The SI / ownership indicator (`SIBox`) visualizes how *your* position relates to the strip:

- White: you are the current `owner` (assumed).
- Purple: you are in `next_controllers` but not the owner (concerned / next-up).
- Orange: your position is in `previous_controllers` (transferred away).
- Grey: you are neither owner, next, nor previous (unconcerned).

When a coordination is pending, the SIBox may switch to split gradients to show:

- Sending: owner side of a pending transfer
- Receiving: target side of a pending transfer

### 1) Initiate a normal transfer (owner only)

Initiate a normal transfer using the `TRF` button in the command bar.

In the current frontend, `TRF` is enabled only when you are the current strip owner (`strip.owner === myPosition`).

When you pick a target controller position, the frontend sends `coordination_transfer_request`.

When the server broadcasts `coordination_transfer_broadcast`, the frontend sets `stripTransfers[callsign]`, and the SIBox displays the pending coordination.

### 2) Owner side: sending / recall (cancel)

While a normal transfer is pending and you are the sending owner:

- Your SIBox shows a white+orange sending-away split.
- Clicking the SIBox cancels the transfer by calling `cancelTransfer()` (`coordination_cancel_transfer_request`).

The strip context menu also exposes `RECALL`, which is only enabled when you own the strip and an outgoing transfer exists.

When the server clears the coordination (reject/free/assume), the frontend clears `stripTransfers` and the SIBox returns to the normal assumed/concerned/unconcerned coloring.

### 3) Target side: receiving / assume (completion)

When a normal transfer is pending and your position matches the target (`stripTransfers[callsign].to === myPosition`):

- Your SIBox becomes clickable and clicking it calls `assumeStrip()` (`coordination_assume_request`).

On `coordination_assume_broadcast`, the frontend clears the pending coordination (`stripTransfers`), and subsequent strip updates update `owner`, `next_controllers`, and `previous_controllers` so the timeline matches for everyone.

## Assuming without a pending transfer

### Direct assume when unowned

If a strip is unowned (`owner` is empty) and your position appears in `next_controllers`, you can assume directly:

- Your SIBox shows the concerned (purple) state.
- Clicking it calls `assumeStrip()` without creating a pending coordination first.

## Tag requests (REQ)

A tag request uses the same pending coordination UI, but with `isTagRequest = true`.

- The `REQ` button is only enabled when the selected strip is in `Bay.Cleared`, the strip is not unowned, you are not the current owner, and there is no active coordination.
- Pressing `REQ` sends `coordination_tag_request`.

While pending:

- The current owner side becomes clickable and clicking the SIBox calls `acceptTagRequest()` (`coordination_accept_tag_request`).
- After acceptance, the pending tag request coordination is cleared by the normal websocket lifecycle.

## Force assume (manual ownership correction)

`FORCE ASSUME` is the escape hatch when things get out of sync (unexpected routing, stale pending coordination, etc.).

In the current frontend:

- `FORCE ASSUME` is a strip context-menu action
- it is disabled when you already own the strip, or when there is an active pending coordination (`stripTransfers[callsign]` exists)

When allowed, the frontend calls `forceAssumeStrip()` (`coordination_force_assume_request`), bypassing the normal "next owner" constraint.

## Freeing a strip (releasing ownership)

The frontend supports freeing via `freeStrip()`:

- it sends `coordination_free_request`
- when `coordination_free_broadcast` is received, the frontend clears any pending coordination indicator (`stripTransfers`)

After the backend processes the free, `owner` becomes empty/unowned and its next/previous controller lists update via normal strip updates.
